MAKING CHANGE THROUGH STEM
Having seen the effects of crime, gangs, and drugs can have on a community, Kevin Akers and Victor Del Carpio sought to see how they could change this. They envisioned a future where any child can be the scientist, doctor, or engineer that they aspired to be, regardless of the socioeconomic status they were born into. Two years later, hundreds of children from impoverished neighborhoods have been introduced to novel STEM concepts. More importantly, however, long lasting mentorships have been created to allow children to succeed.
HACK THE STREETS!​
Through the efforts of Andrew Tran and Rayan Yu, STEM for the Streets will be holding a hackathon on Febraury 22-23. Rayan and Andrew are seeking to do two things: humanize computer science, and introduce novel computer science concepts to the less fortunate. In doing so, more folks will gravitate towards STEM and SFTS will be a step closer to achieving its goal of making STEM more diverse and inclusive.

SFTS Methodology
​We believe that while teaching STEM is important, fostering a long term mentorship to guide students to success holds much more value. Those at SFTS have provided these mentorships to 200+ students.
Real Professionals
At SFTS, we allowed students to be exposed to real experts in the field. In doing so, students are able to grasp a deeper understanding for STEM concepts and gain a more enriching experience
Hands-on Experiments
Teaching subjects through lectures is cool, but having a tangible experience with the learning material is even cooler. At SFTS, we provide hands-on experiments so that students can better grasp the learning material.
