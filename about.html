TEACHING FOR A CHANGE
STEM for the Streets is a student-led non-profit organization aimed at exposing historically disenfranchised and under-served children in NE and SE Washington D.C. to STEM through hands-on science experiments and engaging lesson plans with REAL scientists, engineers, and doctors. Founded on the belief that every child deserves an equal opportunity, STEM for the Streets shows kids that the world is bigger than their neighborhood and that they don't have to become a product of their environment.

OUR STORY
​This non-profit organization could not have been possible without the help of Scott Rechler and others at LearnServe International, a non-profit organization that equips high school students with the tools necessary to make change in their communities.
While Fellows at LearnServe International,  Kevin Akers and Victor Del Carpio worked together in order to form STEM for the Streets. Prototype sessions of STEM for the Streets were held at Achievement Preparatory Academy, a predominately African American D.C. Public Charter School. Students were taught about Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, and Biology and were introduced to Amie Seisay, CEO of Seisay IT Solutions, and Dr. Clayton Lawrence, CEO of Lawrence Executive Alliance of Professionals. STEM for the Streets hopes to expand to more schools in the district in order to provide young minority students with a quality STEM education.
